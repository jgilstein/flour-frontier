Case Study

Where should NAF FSI focus flour over the next ten years?

A structured analytical write up of the Flour Frontier project. The question, the data, the six dimension scoring framework, the four sensitivity lenses, the final ranking, the four phase recommendation, and what comes next.

Section 01 · The Question

What James actually asked

Day one of the internship, James Mueting framed the brief in plain terms. Look ten years out. Tell us where NAF FSI flour growth is going to come from. Back the recommendation with data. Operate within the current five mill, five warehouse network. No new mill capex.

The constraint is the strategy. Knowing that a new mill was off the table forced the analysis toward the moves that are actually available: distributor partnership deepening, warehouse network optimization, toll-milling feasibility, and Account Executive playbook replication.

Section 02 · The Approach

Six analytical phases

Foundation. Top 50 US metros pulled from Circana, Tableau, U.S. census population and projection reports and more. Joined to GM's current mill and warehouse footprint with pairwise distances to surface the supply chain gaps.

Internal performance. GMI Region Full and Account Full data from Tableau, segmented by Account Executive, distributor, metro, and channel. Year over year EQC change at the customer level.

External validation. Circana TTM data at the US Census Region level layered on top, plus the Feb 2026 Flour by DMA cut to localize the picture.

Synthesis. Six dimension scoring framework built and tested. Composite produces the True 5 ranking. Four sensitivity lenses applied as a stress test.

Field pressure test. Sat down with the AEs who actually own the territories. Their context turned numbers into stories and changed the framing in real ways.

Final synthesis. Four phase recommendation with named owners, sequenced by urgency, all working within the current network.

Section 05 · The Network Reality

An Atlanta warehouse changes the equation

Every Phase 2 recommendation in this study is anchored on one specific network reality: GM is most likely going to open a warehouse in Atlanta. That single move is the highest leverage available without any new mill capex.

The Southeast corridor (Atlanta, Charlotte, Nashville, Tampa) currently has no GM warehouse and no nearby mill. An Atlanta warehouse compresses the cost to serve across all four of those metros simultaneously. It also makes deepening the GFS Shepherdsville relationship operationally easier.

Bounded scope, in writing. A new mill would cost roughly $100M and is not in scope. Confirmed in conversation with Cathy Meyer, Director NAF Ingredients. All four recommendations are built to work within the current five mill, five warehouse footprint, with the proposed Atlanta warehouse as the one additive network investment.
Section 06 · The Recommendation

Four phases, zero new mill capex

Phase 1 · Fix Texas Execution
Next 90 Days · FY27 Q1
Sysco Central TX (-38% YoY) and Sysco North TX (-36% YoY) are top 5 customer losses in the entire NAF book and they sit in our #2 ranked metro. The DFW market itself is growing +5.3% YoY, so these are execution gaps not market shifts. Launch a 3 week root cause sprint to categorize the loss (competitive, distributor consolidation, or program execution) and define remediation.
Owner: Gus Patrick (Houston) + Melissa Magsalin (DFW) · James Mueting
Phase 2 · Win the Southeast Corridor
3 to 12 Months · FY27 Q1 - Q4
Atlanta, Tampa, Charlotte, and Nashville cluster in the strongest region per Circana and contain our biggest cold spot. Three concurrent actions: (a) evaluate the proposed Atlanta warehouse cost to serve case (biggest network leverage available, no mill capex), (b) deepen the GFS Shepherdsville partnership where GMI is already up +122.5% YoY under Dean Psyhogios, (c) replicate Josh Cohen's Nashville playbook across Josh Cohen and Jeffrey Santiago territories.
Owner: Josh Cohen + Dean Psyhogios (GFS-KY hub) + Jeffrey Santiago · GM Supply Chain + Melissa Tiemeyer
Phase 3 · Build the Houston Opportunity Case
6 to 18 Months · FY27 Q2 - Q4
Houston is a 60.3M lb DMA where GM holds GMI Index 49, less than half national parity. Reaching parity unlocks roughly 8 to 9 million pounds annually from a single metro. Scope a Houston specific go-to-market plan with Gus Patrick, focused on the independent operator base and the petrochemical workforce concentration.
Owner: Gus Patrick · James Mueting
Phase 4 · Defend the West Without New Capex
12 to 24 Months · FY27 Q4 - FY28
Circana shows West GMI losing 1.1 percentage points of share and down 2.5% in volume. Phoenix is the only True 5 metro with positive year over year performance and the cleanest transport story (370 miles direct truck from Vernon). Double down on the Shamrock Foods relationship, formalize the West Zone Playbook so Shotsie Wilson, Yoel Sharabi, and Nancy Muller's best practices are reproducible, and prevent further share erosion before evaluating any Vernon area capacity decisions.
Owner: Shotsie Wilson + Yoel Sharabi + Nancy Muller · James Mueting
Section 07 · What Comes Next

Handing it off

The most actionable single piece of work for the team to pick up immediately is the Atlanta warehouse evaluation. Cost to serve modeling, customer concentration analysis, and a recommended timing window. That memo, sized to the proposed warehouse's actual lane economics, is the highest leverage deliverable left on the table.

After that, in order: the Texas root cause sprint (Phase 1, owner Gus Patrick and Melissa Magsalin), the GFS Shepherdsville deepening proposal (Phase 2, owner Dean Psyhogios), the Houston opportunity sizing memo (Phase 3, owner Gus Patrick), and the West Zone Playbook v1 (Phase 4, owner Shotsie Wilson).

All four are built to run inside the current network. None of them require new capital. All of them have an owner.
